Our Worlds

🏛️ Tutankhamun

Tutankhamun was a young Egyptian pharaoh from the 18th Dynasty, who ruled around 1332–1323 BC.

His tomb (KV62) was discovered in 1922 in the Valley of the Kings, Luxor, filled with treasures like his famous golden mask and solid gold coffin.

It's one of the greatest archaeological finds in history.

📍 Location: Valley of the Kings, West Bank, Luxor, Egypt

🏛️ The Pyramids of Egypt

The Pyramids of Giza are among the most iconic monuments in the world. Built over 4,500 years ago, they served as royal tombs for the pharaohs of ancient Egypt.

The largest, the Great Pyramid of Khufu, is the only remaining wonder of the ancient world.

📍 Location: Giza Plateau, near Cairo, Egypt

These pyramids showcase the advanced engineering, astronomy, and power of ancient Egyptian civilization.
